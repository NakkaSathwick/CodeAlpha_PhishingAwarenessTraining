1. What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack that uses fake emails, messages, or websites to trick people into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

2. Types of Phishing Attacks

  • Email Phishing: Fake emails prompting you to click links or share info.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted attack on a specific person or organization.
  • Smishing: Phishing through SMS messages.
  • Vishing: Voice calls pretending to be official support.
  • Clone Phishing: Duplicating a real email with malicious edits.

3. How to Recognize Phishing Emails

  • Suspicious or mismatched sender addresses.
  • Spelling or grammar mistakes.
  • Urgent or threatening language.
  • Links that don’t match the sender’s domain.
  • Unusual attachments or requests for personal info.

4. Fake Websites

Attackers create websites that look like trusted ones to steal your credentials. Always check for https://, the padlock icon, and correct domain spelling before entering details.

5. Social Engineering Tactics

  • Urgency: “Your account will be deactivated soon!”
  • Fear: “Your account has been compromised.”
  • Curiosity: “You’ve won a prize!”
  • Authority: “This is from IT support.”

6. Quick Quiz

Question: Which of these is a sign of a phishing email?

A) Professional layout and correct spelling
B) Urgent request for personal information
C) Message from a known contact

7. Summary

  • Always think before you click.
  • Verify sender details and URLs.
  • Never share sensitive information via email.
  • Report phishing attempts immediately.
